# AI Use Policy

**Arbitration Facilitation Platform**
Effective Date: April 2026
Last Updated: April 2026

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This AI Use Policy governs the use of artificial intelligence tools on the Arbitration Facilitation Platform ("the Platform"). It applies to all users of the Platform and to the Platform operator's use of AI in delivering Platform services.

The purpose of this Policy is to ensure transparency about how AI is used, to set clear boundaries on the role of AI in arbitration proceedings, and to ensure that AI tools are used responsibly, safely, and in compliance with applicable legal requirements.

## 2. AI Providers Used

The Platform uses the following AI providers, in order of priority:

| Provider | Model | Role |
|----------|-------|------|
| **OpenAI** | GPT-4o-mini (configurable) | Primary AI provider |
| **Alibaba Cloud DashScope (Qwen)** | Qwen-plus (configurable) | Secondary provider (fallback) |
| **NVIDIA AI** | Nemotron-3-Super-120B-A12B (configurable) | Tertiary provider (fallback) |

The Platform is configured to attempt the primary provider first. If the primary provider is unavailable or returns an error, the Platform automatically falls back to the secondary and then tertiary provider. The same legal accuracy guardrails and data minimisation practices apply regardless of which provider processes a given request.

## 3. What AI Is Used For

AI tools on the Platform are used exclusively for **advisory** purposes in the following workflows:

### 3.1 Document Analysis (Advisory Only)
AI analyses uploaded documents to identify key provisions, potential issues, inconsistencies, or relevant legal considerations. **The AI's analysis is advisory only.** It does not determine the legal weight, admissibility, or interpretation of any document in proceedings.

### 3.2 Arbitration Agreement Analysis (Advisory Only)
AI reviews arbitration clauses and agreements to identify standard or non-standard provisions, potential enforceability issues, and alignment with applicable institutional rules. **All outputs are advisory only and must be reviewed by qualified legal counsel.**

### 3.3 Compliance Checking (Advisory Only)
AI assists with checking procedural steps and documents against applicable rules, including institutional rules and the Kenya Arbitration Act (Cap. 49). Compliance outputs are advisory summaries, not definitive legal compliance determinations.

### 3.4 Legal Research Assistance (Advisory Only)
AI assists users by identifying potentially relevant legal principles, statutes, and precedents. **The Platform implements strict accuracy guardrails requiring the AI to cite only verifiable sources.** However, AI can produce errors and must not be relied upon as a substitute for independent legal research.

### 3.5 Training Content Generation (Advisory Only)
AI assists in generating educational materials, quiz questions, and scenario-based training content for the Platform's training modules. Training content is reviewed before publication.

### 3.6 Case Management Assistance (Advisory Only)
AI may assist with suggesting procedural timelines, drafting template correspondence, and flagging procedural issues. All such outputs require review by the arbitrator or secretariat before acting upon them.

---

## 4. What AI Is Explicitly NOT Used For

The following uses of AI are **prohibited** on the Platform and will not be implemented:

- **Making arbitral decisions:** AI does not decide, recommend the outcome of, or prejudge any dispute.
- **Generating arbitral awards:** AI does not draft, generate, or contribute to the generation of final or partial awards that are presented as arbitral determinations. AI may assist in formatting or drafting where a qualified arbitrator directs and reviews the output, but the award remains entirely the arbitrator's work product.
- **Providing legal advice:** AI outputs are not legal advice. The Platform operator is not a law firm.
- **Appointing arbitrators:** AI does not select or recommend specific individuals for appointment as arbitrators in any case.
- **Determining applicable law:** AI does not make binding determinations of which law governs any dispute.
- **Replacing qualified legal professionals:** AI is a support tool only.

---

## 5. Human Oversight Requirement

**All AI outputs generated by the Platform must be reviewed by a qualified legal professional before being used in, or relied upon in connection with, any arbitration proceedings.**

This requirement is non-negotiable. AI outputs are starting points for professional review, not endpoints. The responsible person (arbitrator, legal counsel, or secretariat, as appropriate) must:

1. Read and critically evaluate any AI-generated analysis;
2. Independently verify any legal citations, case names, or statutory references cited by the AI;
3. Apply their professional judgement before acting on any AI output;
4. Not transmit or file any AI-generated content in proceedings without such review; and
5. Retain professional responsibility for any action taken in reliance on AI outputs.

## 6. AI Hallucination Risk Acknowledgment

AI language models are known to "hallucinate" — that is, to generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect statements, including:

- Fabricated case names, citations, and judgments that do not exist;
- Inaccurate quotations of statutes, treaty articles, or institutional rules;
- Invented legal doctrines or principles;
- Incorrect statements of legal outcomes.

The Platform implements a legal accuracy guardrail (a system-level instruction requiring the AI to cite only verifiable sources and to acknowledge uncertainty) to reduce — but not eliminate — hallucination risk.

**Users must treat all AI outputs as potentially containing errors.** In a legal context, reliance on fabricated citations or invented precedents can cause direct harm to parties. Independent verification of all legal references is mandatory.

## 7. Compliance with EU AI Act Principles

While the Platform is governed by Kenyan law, the Platform operator adopts the principles of the **EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)** as international best practice guidance for responsible AI deployment:

### 7.1 Risk-Based Approach
AI tools used in the legal and arbitration context are treated as high-attention applications requiring enhanced human oversight, consistent with the EU AI Act's approach to AI in legal contexts.

### 7.2 Transparency
Users are informed when AI tools are being used in any Platform feature. AI-generated content is labelled as such.

### 7.3 Human Oversight
The Platform's design prioritises human review and final decision-making authority, consistent with EU AI Act Article 14 requirements for high-risk AI systems.

### 7.4 Accuracy and Robustness
The Platform implements accuracy guardrails and uses fallback AI providers to maximise the reliability of AI outputs, consistent with EU AI Act Article 15 requirements.

### 7.5 Data Governance
The Platform minimises personal data sent to AI providers and applies pseudonymisation where feasible, consistent with privacy-by-design principles.

## 8. Transparency

The Platform is committed to transparency about AI use:

- **Feature labelling:** Platform features that use AI are labelled with an AI disclosure notice.
- **Provider disclosure:** The AI providers used by the Platform are disclosed in this Policy and in the Privacy Policy.
- **Output labelling:** AI-generated content is identified as AI-generated within the Platform interface.
- **Audit trail:** AI interactions are logged in the Platform's audit trail for review purposes.

---

## 9. Data Sent to AI Providers

The Platform transmits the following to AI providers:

- **Document text:** The textual content of documents submitted to AI analysis features.
- **User queries:** Questions or prompts submitted by users to AI research tools.

The Platform does **not** intentionally transmit:
- Full names of parties (where document text can be pre-processed to redact them);
- National identification numbers, passport numbers, or other government-issued identifiers;
- Financial account details;
- Health or medical information.

**Limitation:** Where personal data appears within document text submitted by users, the Platform cannot always automatically identify and remove it before transmission. Users are responsible for considering the sensitivity of documents submitted to AI analysis features.

Data transmitted to AI providers is processed under the providers' terms of service and data processing agreements. The Platform maintains data processing agreements with each provider consistent with the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 requirements for international transfers.

---

## 10. Opt-Out

Parties to arbitration proceedings may request that AI analysis tools not be applied to their case. To exercise this right:

1. Contact the Platform at **platform@arbitration.platform** stating your case reference number and the specific AI features you wish to opt out of.
2. The Platform operator will implement the opt-out within a reasonable time.
3. Opting out of AI analysis will not affect your access to the Platform's other features.

Note: Opting out of AI tools in your case will not retroactively affect AI analysis already completed before the opt-out request was received.
