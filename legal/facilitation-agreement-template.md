## RECITALS

WHEREAS the Platform Operator operates the Arbitration Facilitation Platform (the "Platform"), a technology facilitation service providing case management, document management, virtual hearing support, AI-assisted research tools, and related services for arbitration proceedings;

WHEREAS the Institution is an arbitral institution, professional body, or party organisation that wishes to use the Platform to support its arbitration proceedings and related activities;

WHEREAS the Parties wish to establish the terms on which the Platform Operator will provide facilitation services to the Institution;

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual obligations set forth herein, and for good and valuable consideration the receipt and adequacy of which is hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"Case Data"** means all documents, submissions, communications, hearing records, and other information uploaded to or generated on the Platform in connection with proceedings administered by or through the Institution.

1.2 **"Facilitation Services"** means the technology services described in Article 2 of this Agreement.

1.3 **"Platform"** means the Arbitration Facilitation Platform operated by the Platform Operator, accessible at the URL provided to the Institution.

1.4 **"Proceedings"** means arbitration proceedings, mediation proceedings, or other dispute resolution processes for which the Institution uses the Platform.

1.5 **"Authorised Users"** means the arbitrators, parties, legal counsel, secretariat staff, and administrators authorised by the Institution to access the Platform in connection with Proceedings.

1.6 **"Personal Data"** has the meaning given in the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (Act No. 24 of 2019).

---

## ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF FACILITATION SERVICES

2.1 **Services Provided.** Subject to the terms of this Agreement and payment of applicable fees, the Platform Operator will provide the Institution with access to the following Facilitation Services:

(a) **Case Management:** Digital workflows for case registration, milestone tracking, timeline management, and procedural scheduling;

(b) **Document Repository:** Secure, role-based document storage, upload, retrieval, and versioning;

(c) **Virtual Hearings:** Technology infrastructure for remote hearings, including scheduling, participant management, and recording where authorised;

(d) **AI-Assisted Tools (Advisory Only):** Artificial intelligence tools for document analysis, compliance checking, legal research assistance, and training content generation, all provided on an advisory-only basis as described in the Platform's AI Use Policy;

(e) **Training Modules:** Access to training and certification content for practitioners;

(f) **Analytics:** Reporting and data visualisation tools for case management oversight; and

(g) **User Management:** Tools for the Institution to manage Authorised Users and their roles.

2.2 **Additional Services.** Any services not listed in clause 2.1 require a written amendment to this Agreement signed by both Parties.

---

## ARTICLE 3 — PLATFORM'S ROLE: TECHNOLOGY PROVIDER ONLY

3.1 **Technology Provider Only.** The Platform Operator is a technology service provider only. Nothing in this Agreement, and nothing generated by or through the Platform, constitutes:

(a) Administration of Proceedings in the institutional sense;
(b) An exercise of arbitral authority;
(c) The making, issuing, or enforcing of any arbitral award;
(d) Legal advice to any party;
(e) An appointment of any arbitrator with binding authority; or
(f) Any substantive role in any Proceedings.

3.2 **No Arbitral Authority.** The Platform Operator has no arbitral authority. The Platform Operator is not a party to any arbitration agreement between the Institution and any claimant or respondent. The Platform Operator does not administer Proceedings in the manner of an arbitral institution.

3.3 **Platform as Neutral Tool.** The Platform is a neutral technological tool provided equally to all Authorised Users. The Platform Operator does not take sides in or have any interest in the outcome of any Proceedings.

---

## ARTICLE 4 — INSTITUTION'S ARBITRAL AUTHORITY

4.1 **Full Retention of Authority.** The Institution retains all of its arbitral authority, institutional functions, and administrative responsibilities. Nothing in this Agreement transfers, delegates, or diminishes the Institution's authority over any Proceedings.

4.2 **Institution's Rules Apply.** Proceedings conducted using the Platform remain governed by the Institution's arbitral rules, the applicable arbitration agreement between the parties, and any applicable law. The Platform's workflows are adapted to support — but not replace — the Institution's rules and procedures.

4.3 **Institution's Responsibility.** The Institution remains responsible for:

(a) Ensuring that Proceedings comply with applicable arbitral rules and law;
(b) The appointment of arbitrators and the constitution of tribunals;
(c) All substantive and procedural decisions in Proceedings;
(d) The issuance, certification, and delivery of awards;
(e) Communicating with parties about the conduct of Proceedings; and
(f) Ensuring that Authorised Users comply with the Platform's Terms of Service.

---

## ARTICLE 5 — DATA PROCESSING TERMS

5.1 **Data Controller.** As between the Parties, the Institution is the data controller for Case Data relating to parties, arbitrators, and other participants in Proceedings. The Platform Operator is a data processor in respect of such Case Data, acting on the Institution's instructions.

5.2 **Data Processing Agreement.** The Platform Operator will process Case Data only:

(a) For the purpose of providing the Facilitation Services;
(b) In accordance with the Institution's documented instructions;
(c) In compliance with the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (Act No. 24 of 2019); and
(d) Subject to the security measures described in the Platform's Privacy Policy.

5.3 **Sub-processors.** The Platform Operator uses the following sub-processors to provide the Facilitation Services:

(i) Oracle Cloud Infrastructure — server hosting;
(ii) Neon Database — database hosting;
(iii) OpenAI, Alibaba Cloud DashScope, and NVIDIA AI — AI processing (document text only, with personal identifiers minimised where feasible).

The Platform Operator will inform the Institution of any material changes to sub-processors with reasonable advance notice.

5.4 **Data Security.** The Platform Operator will implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures as described in the Platform's Privacy Policy, consistent with DPA 2019 s. 41.

5.5 **Breach Notification.** In the event of a personal data breach affecting Case Data, the Platform Operator will notify the Institution without undue delay and within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

5.6 **Data Return and Deletion.** Upon termination of this Agreement, the Platform Operator will, at the Institution's election, return all Case Data to the Institution or securely delete it, within a reasonable period not exceeding 30 days. The Platform Operator may retain anonymised or aggregated data for operational and analytical purposes.

5.7 **Audit Rights.** The Institution may, with reasonable prior notice, request information about the Platform Operator's data processing practices or commission an audit of those practices at the Institution's expense.

---

## ARTICLE 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 **Mutual Confidentiality.** Each Party will keep confidential all Confidential Information received from the other Party and will not use it for any purpose other than performing its obligations under this Agreement.

6.2 **Definition.** "Confidential Information" means:

(a) All Case Data;
(b) The commercial terms of this Agreement;
(c) Technical documentation, architecture, and source code relating to the Platform; and
(d) Any information designated in writing as confidential by the disclosing Party.

6.3 **Exceptions.** Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement;
(b) Was already known to the receiving Party at the time of disclosure;
(c) Is independently developed by the receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or
(d) Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority.

6.4 **Survival.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of 5 years, except in relation to Case Data, for which confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely.

---

## ARTICLE 7 — SERVICE LEVELS

7.1 **Pilot Phase Disclaimer.** The Platform is currently in a pilot phase. **The Platform Operator does not guarantee any specific level of uptime, availability, or performance during the pilot phase.** The Platform is provided on a best-efforts basis.

7.2 **Best Efforts Commitment.** The Platform Operator will use commercially reasonable efforts to:

(a) Maintain Platform availability during normal business hours (Nairobi, Kenya time);
(b) Provide advance notice of planned maintenance downtime where possible;
(c) Respond to reported technical issues within 2 business days; and
(d) Restore service as quickly as practicable following unplanned outages.

7.3 **No Uptime Guarantee.** No warranty of uninterrupted or error-free service is made during the pilot phase. The Platform Operator accepts no liability for Proceedings being delayed, disrupted, or otherwise affected by Platform downtime or technical issues.

7.4 **Scheduled Maintenance.** The Platform Operator will use reasonable efforts to schedule planned maintenance outside of normal business hours and to give the Institution at least 48 hours' notice.

7.5 **Post-Pilot.** Service level commitments for any period following the pilot phase will be agreed in writing by the Parties.

---

## ARTICLE 8 — FEES

8.1 **Fees to Be Agreed.** The fees payable by the Institution for the Facilitation Services are as set out in a separate Fee Schedule to be agreed in writing by the Parties and attached to this Agreement as Schedule A.

8.2 **Payment Terms.** Payment terms will be specified in Schedule A.

8.3 **Pilot Phase.** During any agreed pilot phase, the Parties may agree that fees are waived or reduced, as specified in Schedule A.

8.4 **Changes to Fees.** The Platform Operator may revise its fees with 60 days' written notice to the Institution. If the Institution does not accept revised fees, it may terminate this Agreement on 30 days' written notice provided within 30 days of receiving the fee revision notice.

---

## ARTICLE 9 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

9.1 **Platform IP.** All intellectual property rights in the Platform, including software, design, AI models, and training content, remain the property of the Platform Operator.

9.2 **Institution and Case Data IP.** All intellectual property rights in Case Data, Proceedings materials, and any content created by the Institution or its Authorised Users remain the property of the Institution and the respective rights holders.

9.3 **Licence to Platform Operator.** The Institution grants the Platform Operator a limited, non-exclusive licence to process Case Data solely for the purpose of providing the Facilitation Services.

9.4 **No AI Training on Case Data.** The Platform Operator will not use Case Data to train, fine-tune, or improve any AI model without the Institution's prior written consent.

---

## ARTICLE 10 — TERMINATION

10.1 **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated in accordance with this Article.

10.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement on 30 days' written notice to the other Party.

10.3 **Termination for Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately on written notice if the other Party commits a material breach that is not remedied within 14 days of written notice of the breach.

10.4 **Termination for Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters administration, or ceases to carry on business.

10.5 **Effect of Termination.** On termination:

(a) All licences granted under this Agreement cease;
(b) The Institution must pay all outstanding fees;
(c) The Platform Operator will comply with clause 5.6 regarding Case Data; and
(d) Clauses 3, 5, 6, 9, 11, and 12 survive termination.

---

## ARTICLE 11 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

11.1 **Cap.** The Platform Operator's total liability to the Institution under or in connection with this Agreement is limited to the total fees paid by the Institution to the Platform Operator in the 12 months preceding the claim.

11.2 **Exclusions.** Neither Party is liable for indirect, consequential, incidental, or punitive damages, including loss of profits, loss of data, or disruption to Proceedings, arising from this Agreement.

11.3 **Exceptions.** The limitation of liability does not apply to liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or wilful misconduct, or any liability that cannot be limited by applicable law.

---

## ARTICLE 12 — GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Republic of Kenya**.

12.2 **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising from or in connection with this Agreement that cannot be resolved amicably will be submitted to arbitration in Nairobi, Kenya, under the rules of the Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration (NCIA) or such other rules as the Parties may agree in writing. The language of arbitration shall be English. The number of arbitrators shall be one (1).

12.3 **Courts.** Nothing prevents either Party from seeking urgent interim relief from the courts of Nairobi, Kenya.

---

## ARTICLE 13 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement (together with any Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings.

13.2 **Amendments.** This Agreement may only be amended by a written document signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

13.3 **Notices.** Notices under this Agreement must be in writing and delivered by email (with confirmation of receipt) or by registered post to the addresses set out above.

13.4 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

13.5 **Waiver.** No failure or delay by either Party to exercise any right under this Agreement constitutes a waiver of that right.

13.6 **Force Majeure.** Neither Party is liable for failure or delay in performance due to causes beyond its reasonable control.

13.7 **No Assignment.** Neither Party may assign its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that the Platform Operator may assign to a successor entity in a bona fide corporate restructuring.
