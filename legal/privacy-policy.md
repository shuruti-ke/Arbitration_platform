## 2. Categories of Personal Data Collected

### 2.4 Usage and Technical Data
- Browser type, device information, and operating system
- IP addresses and geolocation data (country level)
- Pages and features accessed, timestamps of access
- Error logs and performance data

### 2.5 AI Interaction Data
- Document text submitted to AI analysis features (processed with personal identifiers minimised where possible)
- AI-generated outputs stored for quality review and audit purposes

## 5. Data Retention Periods

We retain personal data for the following periods:

| Category | Retention Period | Basis |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Account data (active users) | Duration of account plus 3 years after account closure | Legitimate interests; legal compliance |
| Case-related data | **7 years after case closure** | Consistent with the Limitation of Actions Act (Cap. 22, Laws of Kenya), which provides a 6-year limitation period for most civil claims, plus a 1-year buffer for late-emerging claims |
| Audit logs and security records | 5 years from creation | Security and legal compliance |
| AI interaction records | 2 years from creation | Quality assurance; audit purposes |
| Authentication logs (IP addresses, login records) | 12 months | Security monitoring |
| Training and certification records | Duration of user's active period plus 5 years | Professional record-keeping |

After the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted or anonymised in accordance with our data disposal procedures.

## 6. Data Subject Rights

Under the Data Protection Act 2019 (ss. 26–32), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

### 6.1 Right of Access (s. 26)
You have the right to obtain confirmation of whether we hold personal data about you and, if so, to receive a copy of that data.

### 6.2 Right to Rectification (s. 27)
You have the right to require correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data held about you.

### 6.3 Right to Erasure (s. 28)
You have the right to request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, where you withdraw consent (if consent is the basis), or where processing is unlawful. This right is subject to our legal obligations to retain certain data (e.g., case records for the retention periods specified above).

### 6.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (s. 29)
You have the right to request that we restrict processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, such as where you contest the accuracy of the data.

### 6.5 Right to Data Portability (s. 30)
Where processing is based on consent or contract and is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

### 6.6 Right to Object (s. 31)
You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will cease such processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

### 6.7 Right to Withdraw Consent (s. 32)
Where we rely on consent as the legal basis for processing, you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

### Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of the above rights, contact us at: **data@arbitration.platform**

We will respond to all requests within the timeframes prescribed by DPA 2019 (generally within 21 days). Where we are unable to comply with a request, we will explain why. If you are dissatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the ODPC.

## 7. International Transfers

The Platform uses the following infrastructure, which involves international transfers of personal data:

### 7.1 Oracle Cloud Infrastructure
The Platform's backend server infrastructure is hosted on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure. Data may be processed in Oracle Cloud data centres outside Kenya.

### 7.2 Neon Database
The Platform's primary database is hosted on Neon (a cloud-hosted PostgreSQL service). Data is stored on Neon's infrastructure, which may be located outside Kenya.

### 7.3 AI Providers
Document text submitted to AI analysis features is transmitted to:
- **OpenAI** (primary provider) — servers located in the United States;
- **Alibaba Cloud DashScope (Qwen)** (secondary provider) — servers located in China/Singapore;
- **NVIDIA AI** (tertiary provider) — servers located in the United States.

We minimise personal identifying information transmitted to AI providers. Document text is processed with personal identifiers removed or pseudonymised where technically feasible.

### 7.4 Safeguards

International transfers are conducted subject to appropriate safeguards as required by DPA 2019 s. 48, including contractual arrangements with processors and, where applicable, reliance on adequacy determinations or standard data protection clauses.

## 8. Security Measures

We implement the following technical and organisational measures to protect personal data:

- **Encryption in transit:** All data transmitted to and from the Platform is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
- **Encryption at rest:** Case documents and sensitive data are stored in encrypted form.
- **Access controls:** Role-based access controls limit data access to authorised users only.
- **Authentication:** User authentication requires strong passwords and supports token-based session management.
- **Audit logging:** All significant actions on the Platform are logged with timestamps and user identifiers.
- **Security headers:** The Platform's API and web interface implement security headers including X-Content-Type-Options, X-Frame-Options, Strict-Transport-Security, and others.
- **Virus scanning:** Uploaded documents are scanned for malware before being stored.
- **Personnel:** Platform personnel with access to personal data are subject to confidentiality obligations.

Despite these measures, no system is completely secure. In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ODPC and, where required, affected data subjects in accordance with DPA 2019 s. 43.
