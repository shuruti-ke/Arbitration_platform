# Platform Charter

**Arbitration Facilitation Platform**
Effective Date: April 2026
Governing Law: Republic of Kenya

---

## 1. Identity and Purpose

The Arbitration Facilitation Platform (hereinafter "the Platform") is a technology facilitation service operated as a neutral digital infrastructure for the support of arbitration proceedings. The Platform is **not** an arbitral institution, arbitral body, or arbitral tribunal. It does not possess, claim, exercise, or purport to exercise any arbitral authority whatsoever.

The Platform exists to provide a common, neutral digital meeting place and case management environment for arbitrators, parties, legal counsel, and secretariat staff engaged in arbitration proceedings that are separately governed by applicable arbitral rules and agreements between the parties.

---

## 2. Explicit Disclaimer of Arbitral Authority

**The Platform has no arbitral authority.**

No action, output, decision, recommendation, or communication generated by or through the Platform — including any AI-assisted analysis, compliance report, document analysis, or automated workflow — constitutes, or shall be construed as constituting:

- An arbitral award or partial award;
- A procedural order with binding effect;
- An appointment of an arbitrator with any authority;
- A ruling on jurisdiction, admissibility, or the merits of any dispute;
- Legal advice to any party;
- Administration of arbitration proceedings in the institutional sense; or
- Any exercise of the functions of an arbitral institution.

All arbitral authority rests exclusively with the arbitrator(s) appointed by the parties or by a competent appointing authority, acting under the applicable arbitration agreement and arbitral rules chosen by the parties.

---

## 3. What the Platform Does

The Platform provides the following technology facilitation services:

### 3.1 Case Management
Structured digital workflows for case registration, timeline management, milestone tracking, document filing, and procedural scheduling. Case management tools assist the parties and tribunal in organising proceedings; they do not direct or control the proceedings.

### 3.2 Document Repository
A secure, role-based document management system allowing authorised users to upload, store, retrieve, version, and share case-related documents. The document repository is a filing tool only. It does not assess the legal weight, admissibility, or relevance of any document.

### 3.3 Virtual Hearings
Technology infrastructure supporting remote hearings via video conference, including scheduling, participant management, and session recording where authorised. The Platform does not conduct hearings; it provides the technological environment within which arbitrators and parties conduct hearings under the arbitrators' direction.

### 3.4 AI-Assisted Research (Advisory Only)
Artificial intelligence tools that assist with document analysis, legal research, compliance checking, arbitration agreement review, and training content generation. **All AI outputs are advisory only.** They are not legal advice, not binding determinations, and must be reviewed and verified by a qualified legal professional before being used in any proceedings. See the AI Use Policy for full details.

### 3.5 Training and Certification
Educational modules, courses, and certification tracking for arbitration practitioners. Training content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute professional legal training regulated by any bar association or professional body.

### 3.6 Analytics and Reporting
Data visualisation and reporting tools that help administrators, arbitrators, and institutions understand case progress, timelines, and platform usage. Analytics are operational management tools only.

---

## 4. What the Platform Does Not Do

The Platform explicitly does **not**:

- Conduct arbitration proceedings;
- Make, issue, ratify, enforce, or recommend arbitral awards;
- Appoint arbitrators with any binding or institutional authority;
- Administer arbitration proceedings as an arbitral institution;
- Provide legal advice to any party or participant;
- Determine the law applicable to any dispute;
- Rule on questions of jurisdiction, arbitrability, or the merits;
- Act as a party to any arbitration;
- Guarantee the enforceability of any award made using the Platform;
- Replace or substitute for qualified legal counsel; or
- Act as a data controller for case content in a manner that gives the Platform operator any authority over the substance of disputes.

## 5. Legal Framework

The Platform operates within the following legal framework:

### 5.1 Kenya Arbitration Act (Cap. 49)
The Platform is designed to support arbitration proceedings conducted under the Arbitration Act (Cap. 49, Laws of Kenya), as amended, which gives effect to the UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration (1985, as amended 2006) in Kenya.

### 5.2 UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration
The Platform's workflows and procedural features are informed by the UNCITRAL Model Law to facilitate proceedings that are compatible with internationally recognised arbitration standards.

### 5.3 Kenya Information and Communications Act (Cap. 411A)
The Platform's electronic communications, data storage, and digital services are conducted in compliance with the Kenya Information and Communications Act (Cap. 411A, Laws of Kenya) and subsidiary legislation thereunder.

### 5.4 Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (Act No. 24 of 2019)
All personal data processed by the Platform is handled in accordance with the Data Protection Act 2019 (Act No. 24 of 2019, Laws of Kenya), administered by the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC). See the Privacy Policy for full details.

### 5.5 Technology Facilitation Platforms
Technology facilitation platforms in the legal and arbitration space — analogous to platforms such as Opus2 and Relativity in international arbitration practice — operate as neutral technology providers. They do not become parties to arbitration agreements and do not acquire arbitral authority by virtue of their role as technology providers. The Platform operator adopts this established model.
